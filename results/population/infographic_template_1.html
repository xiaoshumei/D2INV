How has the overall population size changed over the decades?

The total population increased from approximately 20 million in 1850 to over 281 million in 2000, with the most rapid growth occurring in the 20th century, particularly post-1950.

Continue exploring demographic shifts

How has the age structure of the population evolved over time?

The age distribution shifted from a pyramid shape in 1850 to a more rectangular form by 2000, indicating an aging population with a decreasing proportion of youth and increasing elderly.

Discover gender distribution patterns

What are the sex disparities across different age groups and how have they changed?

Males outnumbered females at younger ages (e.g., age 0 in 1850: 1.48M males vs. 1.45M females), but females had higher survival rates, leading to a female majority in older age groups by 2000 (e.g., age 90: 336K males vs. 1.06M females).

Explore the baby boom phenomenon

When did the baby boom occur and how did it impact population dynamics?

A significant baby boom occurred around 1950, with over 16 million infants (age 0) recorded, compared to lower numbers in earlier decades like 1850 with about 2.9 million.

Understand elderly population growth

How has the elderly population grown over time?

The elderly population (age 65 and above) grew substantially, from around 516,000 in 1850 to over 35 million in 2000, reflecting improvements in healthcare and life expectancy.
