Tech Stock Evolution: 2000-2010

A Decade of Market Dynamics, Bubble Bursts, and Financial Crises

Explore the dramatic shifts in tech stock performances during the tumultuous first decade of the 21st century, featuring market leaders Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, IBM, and Google.

How did the percentage growth of major tech stocks compare over the decade?

From January 2000 to March 2010, Apple's stock price surged by approximately 760%, while Microsoft's declined by about 28%, Amazon increased by around 100%, and IBM rose by nearly 25%.

Continue to discover the dot-com bubble impact

What was the extent of Amazon's stock price decline during the dot-com bubble?

During the dot-com bubble burst, Amazon's stock price plummeted from $64.56 in January 2000 to a low of $5.97 in September 2001, a drop of over 90%.

See how the 2008 financial crisis affected tech stocks

How did the financial crisis impact the stock prices of tech companies?

The 2008 financial crisis caused a significant drop in tech stocks, with Microsoft falling from $35.03 in October 2007 to $16.63 in January 2009, and similar declines observed in others.

Explore Google's impressive growth trajectory

What was Google's stock performance trajectory since its initial public offering?

Google, after its IPO in 2004, saw its stock price rise from $102.37 to $560.19 by March 2010, demonstrating robust growth despite market volatilities.

Witness the shifting rankings of tech giants

How did the relative price rankings of these tech stocks evolve monthly from 2000 to 2010?

The ranking of stock prices shifted over the decade; in 2000, IBM led with the highest price, but by 2010, Apple and Google dominated, reflecting changing market leadership.
