Wildlife Strikes on Aircraft

Analyzing Wildlife Strikes Across Airports, Aircraft, and Flight Phases

Understanding patterns in wildlife-aircraft collisions to improve aviation safety and wildlife management
1

What is the distribution of damage levels caused by wildlife strikes?

The vast majority of wildlife strikes (85.7%) result in no damage to the aircraft, while a small fraction (10.7%) cause minor damage, and (3.6%) cause medium damage.

2

During which phases of flight do wildlife strikes most commonly occur?

Most wildlife strikes occur during approach (53.6%) and climb (21.4%) phases of flight, with fewer incidents during take-off run (14.3%) and descent (7.1%).

3

What sizes and species of wildlife are most frequently involved in strikes?

Small wildlife (41.1%) and medium-sized wildlife (51.8%) are the most common sizes involved in strikes, with large wildlife accounting for 7.1%. Unknown medium birds account for 39.3% of all strikes.

4

Which airports experience the highest frequency of wildlife strikes?

George Bush Intercontinental Airport (5 strikes), Memphis International Airport (5 strikes), and Washington Dulles International Airport (4 strikes) report the highest number of wildlife strikes among the dataset.

5

Which airlines and aircraft models are most frequently involved in wildlife strikes?

Delta Air Lines (10 strikes), Southwest Airlines (7 strikes), and American Airlines (6 strikes) aircraft are most commonly involved in wildlife strikes, with B-757-200 (6 strikes) and B-737-300 (5 strikes) being the most frequent models.

6

How does the time of day affect the frequency of wildlife strikes?

Wildlife strikes predominantly occur during daylight (53.6%), with fewer incidents at night (35.7%) and minimal occurrences at dawn/dusk (10.7%).

7

Which states have the highest frequency of wildlife strikes?

Texas (10 strikes), DC (5 strikes), and Tennessee (5 strikes) have the highest incidence of wildlife strikes among US states, accounting for 35.7% of all incidents.
