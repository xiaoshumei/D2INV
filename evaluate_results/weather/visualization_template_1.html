Weather Patterns in Seattle and New York (2012-2015)

A Comparative Analysis of Climate Trends and Extreme Events

Which city experienced more rainfall annually?
Seattle consistently recorded higher annual precipitation than New York. For example, Seattle had 1,171.5mm of rain in 2012, while New York had 788.3mm. This trend persisted across all four years, with Seattle averaging 1,072.8mm annually vs. New York's 745.6mm.

How did temperature extremes compare between the two cities?
New York experienced more extreme temperature variations. In January 2012, New York recorded a low of -10.6°C, while Seattle's lowest temperature was -4.9°C. Conversely, New York hit 37.2°C in July 2012, compared to Seattle's 33.9°C maximum.

What seasonal weather patterns emerged?
Seattle showed consistent rainfall across all seasons, with no dry months. New York had distinct wet (June-September) and dry (November-March) seasons. Snowfall was significant in New York during winter months, with 33.5cm in January 2012, while Seattle saw minimal snow.

Were there notable extreme weather events?
New York experienced a major snowstorm in January 2012 (33.5cm snowfall) and severe rain in May 2012 (54.4mm in one day). Seattle's most extreme event was a 53.6mm rainday in December 2012.

How did wind patterns differ?
New York had stronger average wind speeds (4.3 m/s) compared to Seattle (3.1 m/s). However, Seattle experienced more consistent wind patterns year-round, while New York's winds peaked during winter storms.
